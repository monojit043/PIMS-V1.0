Section 1

System Overview

PIMS (Piping Isometrics Management System) is a web-based platform that manages the complete lifecycle of piping isometric drawings — from initial upload by the Modeller through multi-stage engineering checks, Group Leader approval, and final lot issuance.

Every isometric drawing (ISO) follows a defined path through the system. Each role in the workflow has a specific set of tasks. The system tracks who has a drawing, what actions have been taken, and what the current status is at all times.

Track any line at any time — use the purple search button (bottom-right of the screen) to look up where any line currently is in the workflow.

Key Concepts

ISO / Drawing / Line: These terms are used interchangeably. Each line number (e.g., P-101-54322-A) corresponds to one isometric drawing file.

Claim: When a user takes ownership of a line for review. A claimed line appears in "My Tasks".

Lot: A batch of Final-approved lines grouped together for issuance. Once a lot is created, those lines are moved to the Issued Lots folder.

Inch Dia / Inch Meter: Pipe diameter and length metrics uploaded separately via Excel and shown alongside the drawing in review screens.

Section 2

Roles & Responsibilities

Each user is assigned one or more roles per project and unit. A single person can hold multiple roles (e.g., a senior engineer may be both PC and GL).

Code Full Name Primary Responsibility Key Actions
Modeller ISO Modeller Uploads isometric drawing files into PIMS Upload ISO, Re-upload revised ISO after comments
PC Process Checker Reviews drawing for process engineering compliance Add comment, Upload marked file, Annotate PDF, No Comments, Send to Modeller (GL Commented lines)
MC Material Checker Reviews drawing for material specification compliance Same as PC
SC Stress Checker Reviews drawing for stress and structural compliance Add comment, Upload file, Annotate, No Comments
GL Group Leader Final technical approval authority for the unit Approve → Final, Comment back to PC/SC, Escalate to SGL, Create and issue lots
SGL Senior Group Leader Handles escalated cases above GL authority Approve → Final, Comment back to checkers (with delegation)
Role assignments are managed by the SGL via Admin → Projects → Assign Roles. A user who is not assigned to a project/unit will not see lines from that unit in their tasks.

Step-by-Step Explanation

1
Modeller Uploads ISO  Uploaded

The Modeller uploads the isometric drawing PDF through Upload ISO. The system stores the file and creates a drawing record. Status becomes Uploaded.

All checkers assigned to that project/unit receive a notification in their Checker Notifications inbox. The line appears as available to claim.

2
Checkers Claim & Review  Under Review

PC and MC see the line in their notifications and claim it. Once claimed, it appears in their My Tasks inbox. Status becomes Under Review.

When a checker opens a line, they see the drawing details, Line Engineering Data, Inch Dia/Meter, and LMS data. They choose one of four comment options:

Comment Options
→ Add Comment — types a text comment about an issue found
→ Upload Commented File — uploads a marked-up PDF with annotations
→ Annotate PDF In-Browser — opens the ISO in the built-in PDF annotator, saves markups, then posts
→ No Comments — drawing is correct; no changes needed. PC must verify a 6-point safety checklist before this option is accepted
3
All Checker Claims Completed — Two Paths

Once every checker (PC, MC, and any SC) has submitted their response, the system evaluates:

Path A — Any checker raised a comment
→ Status becomes Comments Received. The Modeller is notified to re-upload an incorporated revision. The cycle repeats from Step 1.
Path B — All checkers gave "No Comments"
→ SC Gate: If Stress Checkers are assigned to the unit, the system waits for an SC to also submit No Comments before advancing. PC's "No Comments" automatically notifies the SC pool (or the previous SC if they reviewed this line before).
→ Once SC also submits No Comments (or if no SC is assigned), status becomes Ready for GL. The GL is notified.
4
GL Reviews  Ready for GL

The GL sees the line in their My Tasks. They open it, review the ISO file and all checker history, and choose one of three actions:

GL Options
✓ Approve → Final — drawing is technically correct. Status becomes Final. Line moves to GL's Final Isometrics folder. Go to Step 7.
! Add Comment (Route to PC or SC) — GL has a concern. Status becomes GL Commented. GL chooses whether to route back to PC or SC. Go to Step 5.
↑ Send to SGL — GL escalates the decision to the Senior Group Leader. Status becomes Ready for SGL. Go to Step 6.
5
GL Commented — PC or SC Responds  GL Commented

The PC or SC (whoever GL routed to) gets the line back in their My Tasks. They see the GL comment in the history. They have three options:

PC / SC Response Options
→ Send to Modeller (Direct) — PC/SC agrees with GL's comment. The line is routed immediately to the Modeller with status Comments Received for re-upload. The Modeller incorporates all GL and checker comments. Cycle repeats from Step 1.
→ Edit & Send to Modeller — PC/SC adds their own notes (and optionally an edited PDF) before sending to the Modeller. Same outcome as above but with extra guidance for the Modeller.
→ Add Comment / No Comments (POST button) — PC/SC disagrees with GL or has already resolved the concern. Posts their own comment back. If "No Comments" is used, the drawing goes back through the all-done check (Step 3) and may advance back to GL. This is the mechanism for PC/SC to push back to GL with reasoning.
6
SGL Reviews  Ready for SGL

The SGL sees the line in their My Tasks. They review the full history and choose:

SGL Options
✓ Approve → Final — status becomes Final. Proceeds to Step 7.
! Add Comment — SGL delegates the comment back to PC, MC, or SC (selectable). Status becomes SGL Commented – Delegated. The chosen checker receives the line for action.
7
Final Isometrics  Final

Approved lines appear in the GL's Final Isometrics folder with Inch Dia and Inch Meter visible. The GL selects one or more lines (from the same Job/Unit) and clicks + Create Lot from Selected.

A confirmation toast shows the lot number, total lines, total Inch Dia, and total Inch Meter. The lines immediately move into the Issued Lots folder.

8
Issued Lots

Issued lots appear in the sidebar under Issued Lots → Job → Unit → Lot N. Clicking a lot opens its detail view showing all lines with Rev, Zone, Stress Critical, and date. Each line row is clickable to open the final ISO PDF directly.

Section 4

Role-by-Role Guide

Select your role below for a step-by-step guide on what to do when you log in.

Modeller — What to do when you log in

  • 1Go to Inbox → My Tasks. Any lines showing Comments Received are waiting for you to re-upload a revised drawing.
  • 2Click the line to open it. Review the History table — all checker and GL comments are listed with their files.
  • 3Incorporate all comments into your CAD model, produce a new ISO PDF.
  • 4Go to Upload ISO in the left sidebar. Select the correct Job, Unit, Zone, and upload the new PDF. The system auto-increments the upload count (e.g., R0-1 → R0-2).
  • 5The line returns to the checkers who previously reviewed it. They are notified automatically.
  • 6To upload a brand-new line for the first time, also use Upload ISO. All available checkers will be notified.
Accepted file format: PDF only. Maximum file size: 50 MB.

Process Checker / Material Checker — What to do when you log in

  • 1Check Checker Notifications in the sidebar for new lines available to claim. Click a line to claim it — it moves to your My Tasks.
  • 2In My Tasks, click any claimed line to open the review screen. You will see: Line Engineering Data, Inch Dia/Meter, LMS data, History, and the comment options panel.
  • 3Review the ISO PDF (click the line row in the task table to open it). Check it against the engineering data shown.
  • 4If you found issues: select Add Comment, Upload Commented File, or Annotate PDF In-Browser. Select the target Modeller if known. Click POST.
  • 5If the drawing is correct: select No Comments. A 6-point safety checklist will appear — tick all items to enable the POST button. Click POST.
  • 6If you need a Stress Check: tick ISO is good for supporting and click Good for Supporting to notify the SC pool.
  • 7GL Commented lines: if a line comes back with GL Commented status, you will see an orange action panel at the top. Choose: Send to Modeller, Edit & Send to Modeller, or use the comment options to push back to GL.

Stress Checker — What to do when you log in

  • 1Check Checker Notifications for lines available for stress check. These are lines where PC has already submitted "No Comments" and the drawing has been flagged for stress review.
  • 2Claim the line. It appears in My Tasks.
  • 3Open the line and review the ISO. Engineering data and LMS data are shown in the panel.
  • 4If issues found: use Add Comment, Upload File, or Annotate. Click POST.
  • 5If the drawing is correct from a stress perspective: select No Comments, complete the checklist, click POST. The line then advances to GL.
  • 6GL Commented lines routed to SC: same as PC — orange action panel appears. Send to Modeller directly or push back to GL.

Group Leader — What to do when you log in

  • 1Check GL Notifications and My Tasks for lines with status Ready for GL.
  • 2Click a line to open the GL Review screen. Review the history table (all checker comments are visible), the ISO PDF, and the engineering data strip.
  • 3If the drawing is technically correct: click ✓ Approve → Final. The line moves to your Final Isometrics folder.
  • 4If you have a comment: select Add Comment, Upload File, or Annotate PDF. Choose whether to route to Process Checker or Stress Checker. Click POST Comments → PC/SC.
  • 5If it requires SGL approval: click → Send to SGL.
  • 6Creating a lot: click Final Isometrics in the sidebar. Select lines using the checkboxes. Click + Create Lot from Selected. A toast confirms the lot number and inch totals. Lines move to Issued Lots.
  • 7Viewing issued lots: expand Issued Lots in the sidebar. Navigate to the correct Job → Unit → Lot. Click to view all lines and open their ISO files.

Senior Group Leader — What to do when you log in

  • 1Check My Tasks for lines with status Ready for SGL. These have been escalated by the GL.
  • 2Open the line. The full history including the GL's escalation reason is visible.
  • 3If approved: click ✓ Approve → Final. Status becomes Final.
  • 4If comments needed: select Add Comment or Upload File. Use the Delegate to Roles section to choose which checker (PC, MC, or SC) should receive and action the comment. Click POST.
  • 5The delegated checker is notified and the line appears in their My Tasks for action.

Status Reference

Every drawing has a status that reflects its current position in the workflow. The status is always visible in the task table and in the Track Line modal.

Status Meaning Who acts next How it gets here
Uploaded Drawing has just been uploaded or re-uploaded PC MC Modeller uploads a new or revised ISO
Under Review At least one checker has claimed the line PC MC SC Checker claims from notification pool
Comments Received Checkers have posted comments; drawing needs revision Modeller All checkers done; at least one raised a comment
Ready for GL All checkers gave No Comments; awaiting GL review GL All active claims completed with No Comments (+ SC gate passed)
GL Commented GL has reviewed and raised a comment PC or SC GL posts a comment (routed to PC or SC)
Ready for SGL GL has escalated the drawing to the SGL SGL GL clicks "Send to SGL"
SGL Commented – Delegated SGL has commented and delegated action to a checker PC / MC / SC SGL posts comment with delegation
Final Drawing has been approved; ready for lot issuance GL (lot creation) GL or SGL clicks "Approve → Final"
Section 6

Frequently Asked Questions

I don't see any lines in My Tasks. What should I check?
First check Checker Notifications or GL Notifications in the sidebar — new lines that are available to claim appear there, not directly in My Tasks. If you see nothing there either, your role may not be assigned to the relevant project/unit. Contact your SGL to confirm your role assignment under Admin → Projects.
I posted comments but the line is still in my My Tasks.
This happens when other checkers have not yet completed their review of the same line. The system waits for all active claims to be completed before advancing the status. Once every checker submits, the line will leave My Tasks automatically.
Where is a specific line right now?
Use the purple search button (magnifier icon, bottom-right of the screen). Type the Job No and Line No to see the current status, who holds it, lot information, and the last 5 activity entries.
What is Inch Dia and Inch Meter?
Inch Dia is the nominal pipe diameter expressed in inches. Inch Meter is the pipe length in meters weighted by diameter — a measure used for estimating scope and progress. These values are uploaded separately via an Excel file by the project admin. They are shown in the task review screens and in the Final Isometrics table.
What is LMS data?
Line Mounted Summary (LMS) data contains information about instruments, valves, and other equipment mounted on a specific line. It is uploaded via an Excel file and is visible in the review screen for checkers, GL, and SGL. It helps reviewers verify that all in-line items are correctly shown on the isometric.
A GL Commented line is back in my inbox. What should I do?
Open the line from My Tasks. An orange GL COMMENTED banner will appear at the top. Read the GL's comment in the History table. Then choose:
  • Send to Modeller — if GL is right and the drawing needs to change, send it directly to the Modeller for re-upload.
  • Edit & Send to Modeller — same as above but you can add your own notes for the Modeller before sending.
  • POST with No Comments — if you believe the drawing is already correct and GL's concern can be addressed without a redraw, post No Comments. This pushes the line back toward GL.
What happens after a lot is created?
The selected lines are immediately moved from the Final Isometrics table into the Issued Lots sidebar. The lot appears under the correct Job → Unit path. Clicking the lot shows all lines in it. Each line row is clickable to open the final approved ISO PDF directly.
Can a line be in Final Isometrics more than once?
No. Once a line is in an issued lot, it is removed from the Final Isometrics view. If the same line is later revised and goes through the workflow again (new revision), it will appear again in Final Isometrics after the new revision is approved.
